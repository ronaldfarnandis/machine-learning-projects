This project trains a machine learning model to churn customer prediction based on Churn_Modelling dataset which includes customer demographic and financial data including credit score, age, tenure, balance, number of products, and activity status. The target variable is Exited which shows whether a customer has moved out of the bank (1) or remained (0). This is aimed at identifying churning customers to ensure that the business measures preventive measures to retain them.

In the preprocessing phase, data columns like RowNumber, CustomerId and Surname, among others, were eliminated. Categorical questions such as Geography and Gender were turned to numbered form by use of one-hot encoding. The data was then divided into training and testing data sets and features scaling was done to standardize the numeric variables. The classification task was done using a Random Forest classifier. Random Forest is the ensemble algorithm that produces a series of decision trees and their predictions are combined to enhance the accuracy and minimize overfitting.

The model attained an accuracy of about 86 percent. The confusion matrix and the classification report show that the model is very effective in the identification of the customers that remain or stay, and it identifies almost half of all the customers that churn. Despite the fact that the churn recall can be enhanced further, the model effectively proves the way machine learning methods can be utilized to determine the customer behavior. Businesses are likely to lose customers because of such an arrangement but can curb this effect with a well-targeted retention approach.
